About me

My name is Christina Pollalis and I graduated from Wellesley College in 2016, where I studied Political Science and Computer Science. I am currently working in the Wellesley Human-Computer Interaction Lab as a Research Fellow doing User Experience research, project management, and software development.

Working in the Wellesley HCI lab has taught me how to design and prototype user interfaces, develop them using the appropriate software tools, and evaluate them by conducting in-person and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.

My current responsibilities in the lab involve leading project design meetings, managing and guiding 3 teams of 11 undergraduate students total, working on the projects, and collaborating with researchers in other universities. This also includes being responsible for deliverables, work allocation, coordination and in-person and remote communications in each team. I have also authored publications and presented the research we are doing at conferences.
